Renewable Energy

Renewable energies, also called clean or alternative energies, are those that come from nature and are naturally renewed in a human time, and cause low environmental impact. Below we have some of them.

Wind

Wind energy is the transformation of wind into electrical energy through wind turbines driven by wind,being one of the fastest growing renewable energy sources in the world today.

One of the main negative points of this energy and the impacts on local fauna, especially bats and birds, which control pests. They crash into the propellers and die.


Solar


Solar energy is an alternative, renewable and sustainable source of energy that comes from the electromagnetic radiation emitted daily by the sun. This energy can be used by different technologies, such as solar heaters, photovoltaic panels and solar power plants.

It has a high acquisition cost, it does not generate energy at night or in how cloudy the sky, and does not have much incentive in Brazil.


Water


Hydropower uses river water as a source of renewable energy to generate electricity. The potential energy of moving water, which can also be accumulated in the hydroelectric reservoir, is captured by turbines and generators that transform mechanical energy into electricity.

Although water energy allows electricity to be generated without the emission of polluting gases, the construction of hydroelectric reservoirs has serious environmental impacts due to flooding of large areas that destroy local fauna and flora, and causes the displacement of riverine communities.

The Brazilian electricity sector is still highly dependent on water energy, which accounts for almost 60% of the country’s electricity matrix. This brings problems in periods of drought, when low hydroelectric production needs to be compensated by thermoelectric, more expensive and polluting. The extra cost with these thermal plants is passed on to consumers through the tariff flags applied in the electricity bill.


Geothermal


Geothermal energy is a renewable and clean source of heat produced continuously in the inner layer of the Earth (mantle), which can be used to heat homes and businesses or to generate electricity through geothermal plants.

Geothermal plants use pipelines inserted in soil perforations to capture this natural steam or to inject water and generate steam, which is directed to turbines and generators that generate, respectively, mechanical and electrical energy.

Unfortunately, it is a very expensive and not very profitable energy, as it requires high structural investments and its efficiency is low.


Biomass


Biomass is a renewable energy source that works through the production of heat and electricity through the direct burning of organic materials (plant and animal waste) or gases released by its decomposition, in addition to the production of biofuels, such as ethanol.

In a biomass thermoelectric plant, the different types of organic matter are burned to generate heat and heat water, producing high-pressure steam that powers the turbines and electric generators. Biomass is considered a renewable energy source, because although the burning of organic matter produces CO2 gases, these are absorbed by the photosynthesis of plants during their growth.


Wave


Wave power allows electricity to be generated through the movement of sea waves (up and down), being a renewable, alternative, and clean energy source.

The movement of waves can be captured in different ways, but all working on the basis of turbines or hydraulic pumps that, when fed by the movement of waves, generate mechanical energy that is then converted into electricity through generators.

But since not everything is perfect, wave energy has high equipment costs, causes removal of marine fauna due to equipment noise, and also has a negative visual impact on the coast.


Tidal


Tidal energy, is electricity generated by the unevenness of seawater caused by the tidal phenomenon. It is a clean energy, which does not produce polluting waste to the environment and uses a renewable source, which is water.

Generally, propellers are used to capture the kinetic energy of the displacement of maritime currents (just as a wind turbine captures the displacement of winds), transforming it into mechanical energy that is then sent to the generator to turn electric energy.

Despite this, equipment costs are still very high, and there are impacts on marine ecosystems in the areas where the tidal power plants are installed.



Non-renewable Energy

Non-renewable energy sources are those from resources with limited reserves in nature and that take millions of years to recover, and may become scarce if used irresponsibly. Below we have some examples of them.


Oil


Oil is still the most used non-renewable energy source in the world energy matrix from its derivatives, which can generate heat and electricity, and serve as fuel for transportation.

Power generation from petroleum occurs with the burning of petroleum derivatives such as fuel oil, diesel oil, ultraviscous oil and refinery gas in boilers. The steam generated by burning drives turbines that start the production of electricity.

Due to the high concentration of carbon in its composition, the burning of oil releases polluting gases into the atmosphere, such as CO2, contributing to the destruction of the ozone layer and the increase of the greenhouse effect.


Mineral Coal


Coal is also a non-renewable and polluting source widely used, occupying the second place in the world matrix. It is the result of high pressure and heat applied to the wood deposits of forests and marshes that have been covered by layers of sediment for about 200 million years.

Coal is used for generating electricity in thermoelectric plants or for producing heat. It has also been widely used as fuel, as in locomotives and steam-powered boats.


Natural Gas


Natural gas is a gaseous type of hydrocarbon (formed only by carbon and hydrogen) derived from the decomposition of organic matter and constitutes a non-renewable energy source. It is found in underground reserves, associated or not with oil, in addition to biogas originated by burning biomass (sugarcane bagasse).

Today, natural gas is the third largest non-renewable source of the world energy matrix, used in thermoelectric power generation, in stoves and heating systems in homes and companies, or even as an alternative fuel for vehicles. Without sustainable use, their reserves can run out in less than 100 years.


Nuclear Energy


Nuclear energy is a non-renewable source for electricity generation from the division of the nucleus (nuclear fission) of the atoms of radioactive elements, which multiply releasing a lot of energy. The heat from this energy heats water and generates pressurized steam that powers the turbines and generators to produce electricity.

Although nuclear power plants can produce clean energy without the emission of greenhouse gases, they generate radioactive waste that needs to be stored in order not to contaminate the environment, as well as presenting risks of accidents, such as the famous case of Chernobyl.

Nuclear energy is also considered a non-renewable source, because the radioactive elements used in its generation have limited reserves and take millions of years to recover. The most used is uranium, which undergoes previous processes of purification and concentration (enrichment).



What you can do


1 - Value natural lighting


Value natural lighting Among the ways to save electricity, the appreciation of natural lighting is one of the most practical and beneficial. Through this initiative, it is possible to optimize the lighting capacity of environments, reducing the need to turn on artificial lights and also have more visual comfort and well-being by increasing contact with sunlight.

For this, avoid blocking natural light made by more robust furniture such as bookcases and wardrobes. It is also recommended to paint the walls with light colors, which help to better reflect the sunlight.

Still, betting on the use of mirrors on the walls, and even mirrored furniture, is a smart strategy, because they, in addition to generating a sense of amplitude, help to illuminate the rooms, reflecting light.


2 - Change your lamps


When thinking about ways to save electricity you need to take into account the exchange of lamps in your home. This is because this type of lighting accounts for about 20% of the total energy consumption of a house. Therefore, it is important to choose the models that demand less consumption.

Incandescent lamps are among the models that must be changed, since they are the ones that consume the most energy. On the other hand, LED lamps are more cost-effective, as they are 80% more economical than incandescent and 30% more economical than fluorescent.


3 - Remove the electronic devices from the outlet


There are some people who think it’s a myth, but the fact is that taking electronics out of the socket can actually reduce electricity consumption. Among the equipment that most generate this type of cost are televisions and microwave ovens.

If you have doubts about the effectiveness of this action, know that, it is not news that connected consume too much. Make a test. Did you know that only one cable set-top box that is used for a few hours a day, but stays the rest of the time in the socket, can generate an additional cost of almost one hundred reais annually?


4 - Pay attention to the state of appliances


Pay attention to the state of appliances Keeping your appliances in good condition is essential to not generate waste of electricity. Therefore, it is important to adopt practices such as checking if the refrigerator rubber has good sealing and regularly clean filters from washing machines and air conditioners.


5 - Decrease the use of appliances such as electric iron, shower and washing machin


In the composition of a light bill, the use of washing machine and shower are among the champions in raising costs.

To reduce your consumption, give preference to ironing more clothes at a time than ironing one piece each alloy of the iron. When using the shower, whenever possible, use the Summer function and try to reduce the time of your baths. For the case of the washing machine, also try to join several pieces to use full capacity of the equipment, instead of washing few pieces at a time.

With small attitudes, it is possible to achieve great results! These are simple ways to save energy, reduce your electricity bill and still have more sustainable attitudes in your day to day.
